About PeriDocs

Ethical and design philosophy

PeriDocs’ name is inspired by Polanyi’s Paradox: much human knowledge is tacit and cannot be fully formalized. PeriDocs bridges algorithmic detection and nuanced understanding without pretending to replace human judgment. PeriDocs itself is intended to serve as a safe, accessible, and easily navigable space for users to explore our database and find credible, actionable, AND personally relevant information which effectively applies to their unique struggles and experiences. At PeriDocs, we recognize and deeply admire the collective effort put in by the multitude of individuals and groups aiming to foster wellbeing regardless of personal affiliation. It is our mission to uplift their hard work by matching the right people with the right projects. PeriDocs prioritizes transparency, accountability, and ethical reflection. Algorithms, repetition detection, paraphrase mirrors, and crisis flags respect user privacy while providing meaningful educational insights. The interface avoids addictive engagement patterns and encourages careful exploration, reflection, and depth. The platform does not generate therapeutic advice or diagnoses. Instead, it analyzes submitted text and finds relevant, previously verified material to help users interpret or contextualize their own writing.

System limitations

PeriDocs is not a mental health service and does not replace professional care. Analysis operates only on the text you submit and cannot infer context outside the words written. Similarity, sentiment, and entity detection are algorithmic and intended for educational reflection only. Results should never be relied upon for clinical or urgent decisions. Algorithms may be fallible and reflect patterns in your writing rather than absolute truths.

How analysis works

When you submit text, embeddings.py processes it using MiniLM and RoBERTa sentence embeddings (all-MiniLM-L6-v2) and internal helpers like NumPy and FastAPI. The system breaks text into tokens (words, numbers, symbols) and identifies:

  • Entities & tokens: names, places, organizations, or colloquial markers such as “idk”, “bruh”, or other casual expressions. Detected tokens may be highlighted on the submit-success page for transparency.
  • Sentiment and emotion anchors: emotional tone is calculated using lexicons of words and phrases for positive and negative feelings, intensifiers, deintensifiers, and repeated words. Users receive a JSON-visible breakdown across six basic emotions with normalized intensity scores.
  • Semantic similarity: compares your text with prior entries using embeddings (all-MiniLM / RoBERTa) to detect repeated ideas, related concepts, or phrasing patterns. Cosine similarity captures synonyms, typos, and paraphrases. Fuzzy or edit-distance highlights are optionally used to show recognized proper nouns, slang, or colloquial terms to the user for transparency.

Your saved entry

Each journal entry is assigned a unique SHA256-based 8-character ID (for example, 5edae32d) to track it privately without storing identifiable information. You are responsible for saving this ID. Entries are stored securely and visible only to authorized PeriDocs staff for moderation, safety review, or research into system performance. User text is never shared publicly.

Paraphrase Mirror: A simplified reflection of your text, often prefixed with "You wrote: …". It removes filler words, repeated words, and extra punctuation while prioritizing sentences with stronger emotional content. This feature is meant as a reflective mirror rather than a precise summary.

Repetition Multiplier: Measures the amount of repetition within the entry you just submitted.

Matched Excerpt & Source ID: Displays the most similar prior entry to your current text with its unique ID. Matches are calculated using embeddings and paraphrased to preserve privacy.

Embedding vectors: Numeric vectors representing your text for semantic similarity detection. These vectors are maintained in full internally to preserve the nuance and relational patterns of your words; they are never truncated or flattened for storage, and are never shared publicly. Maintaining embeddings before redaction allows PeriDocs to identify related entries across different users without exposing private content, providing semantic linking for educational reflection while keeping raw text confidential.

Emotion and sentiment features

PeriDocs detects nuanced emotional tone using large lexicons of words and phrases for positive and negative feelings, intensifiers, deintensifiers, fillers, and colloquial expressions. The system accounts for context, repeated words, and negations to provide a balanced and normalized emotion profile. You receive a breakdown across six basic emotions and a sentiment polarity score, which is mapped to negative, neutral, or positive labels with transparent weighting, allowing users to understand how the system derived each score. This helps users understand patterns in their emotional expression over time.

Privacy and safety

PeriDocs adheres to strict privacy protocols. Each submission receives a public 8-character hash ID for general browsing and a pseudonymized back-end identifier derived from your IP using HMAC for analytics and rate-limiting. The server sees your IP on each request, but human staff do not. Raw IPs are encrypted and stored only for the sake of retroactive retreival of user entries in the event of cooperation with law enforcement. Decrypted records are accessible only to authorized personnel, and all access actions are logged. Users may request content removal by emailing peridocs@protonmail.com with the entry ID. Aggregated anonymized data may persist for internal analytics.

Crisis detection

Submissions indicating risk of harm to self or others are flagged immediately. Users see a safety notification directing them to professional help rather than saving the entry. In the U.S., dial 911 for emergencies or 988 for the Suicide & Crisis Lifeline. Outside the U.S., users are referred to local emergency services.

Contact

Questions or concerns about entries, privacy, or system operation can be sent to peridocs@protonmail.com.
