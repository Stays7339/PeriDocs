About PeriDocs

PeriDocs is an educational mental health platform designed to help users reflect on their personal writing by connecting it to a curated library of reviewed materials, lived experiences, and community insights. The platform does not generate therapeutic advice or diagnoses. Instead, it analyzes submitted text and finds relevant, previously verified material to help users interpret or contextualize their own writing.

System limitations

PeriDocs is not a mental health service and does not replace professional care. Analysis operates on text only and cannot infer context outside the words written. Similarity, sentiment, and entity detection are algorithmic and for educational reflection only. Results should never be relied upon for clinical or urgent decisions. Algorithms may be fallible.

How analysis works

When you submit text, nlp.py processes it using spaCy (en_core_web_sm) and MiniLM sentence embeddings (all-MiniLM-L6-v2), along with internal helpers such as NumPy and FastAPI. The system breaks text into tokens (words, numbers, symbols) and identifies:

  • Entities: names, places, organizations, or colloquial markers (e.g., “idk”, “bruh”).
  • Sentiment: emotional tone scored on a 0–100 scale and categorized into buckets (negative, neutral, positive) using a custom heuristic.
  • Semantic similarity: compares your text with prior entries to detect repeated ideas or related content.

Your saved entry

Each journal entry is assigned a unique SHA256-based ID (e.g., 5edae32d) to track it privately without storing identifiable information. You are responsible for saving this ID. Entries are stored securely and visible only to authorized PeriDocs staff for moderation, safety review, or research into system performance. User text is never shared publicly.

Paraphrase Mirror: A deterministic condensation of your text, often prefixed with "You wrote: …". It removes filler words, repeated words, and punctuation, prioritizing sentences with stronger sentiment. This is intended as a reflective mirror rather than a perfect summary.

Repetition Multiplier: Measures similarity to previous entries via cosine similarity of sentence embeddings. Values start at 1.0 and increase with semantic overlap. This highlights repeated ideas or closely related content in your writing history.

Matched Excerpt & Source ID: Displays the closest prior entry to your current text, along with its unique ID. Computed algorithmically using sentence embeddings; paraphrased to ensure privacy.

Average Sentence Length & Count: Informational metrics calculated from your text to provide insight into writing style and structure.

Embedding: Numeric vectors representing your text for similarity detection. These vectors are never shared publicly and are used solely for internal reflection and analysis.

Privacy and safety

PeriDocs adheres to strict privacy protocols. Routine submissions store a public 8-character hash ID for general-public browsing and a back-end (PeriDocs Staff Only) pseudonymized identifier derived from your IP using HMAC, enabling operational analytics and rate-limiting without retaining raw addresses. The server sees your IP on each request, but human staff never do. Raw IPs are encrypted and stored only if a submission triggers a verified safety or legal escalation. Access to decrypted records is restricted to authorized legal personnel, and every action is logged for audit purposes. Users may request content removal by emailing peridocs@protonmail.com with the entry ID, which revokes the license granted in the Terms of Service. Aggregated, anonymized data may persist for internal analytics.

Crisis detection

Submissions indicating risk of harm to self or others are flagged immediately. In such cases, users are shown a safety notification directing them to professional help rather than saving the entry. In the U.S., dial 911 for emergencies or 988 for the Suicide & Crisis Lifeline. Outside the U.S., users are referred to local emergency services.

Ethical and design philosophy

PeriDocs is designed for transparency, accountability, and ethical reflection. Algorithms, repetition detection, paraphrase mirrors, and crisis flags respect user privacy while providing meaningful educational insight. The interface avoids engagement optimization, favoring clarity, slow exploration, and depth. PeriDocs’ name is inspired by Polanyi’s Paradox: much human knowledge is tacit and cannot be fully formalized. The system bridges explicit, algorithmically detectable features and nuanced context without pretending to replace human judgment.

Contact

Questions or concerns about entries, privacy, or system operation can be sent to peridocs@protonmail.com.
