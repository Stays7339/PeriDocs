About PeriDocs

PeriDocs is an educational mental health platform designed to help users reflect on their personal writing by connecting it to a curated library of reviewed materials, lived experiences, and community insights. The platform does not generate therapeutic advice or diagnoses. Instead, it analyzes submitted text and finds relevant, previously verified material to help users interpret or contextualize their own writing.

System limitations

PeriDocs is not a mental health service and does not replace professional care. Analysis operates only on the text you submit and cannot infer context outside the words written. Similarity, sentiment, and entity detection are algorithmic and intended for educational reflection only. Results should never be relied upon for clinical or urgent decisions. Algorithms may be fallible and reflect patterns in your writing rather than absolute truths.

How analysis works

When you submit text, nlp.py processes it using spaCy (en_core_web_sm) and MiniLM sentence embeddings (all-MiniLM-L6-v2), along with internal helpers like NumPy and FastAPI. The system breaks text into tokens (words, numbers, symbols) and identifies:

  • Entities: names, places, organizations, or colloquial markers such as “idk”, “bruh”, or other casual expressions.
  • Sentiment: emotional tone is detected using a highly sensitive algorithm that recognizes joy, sadness, anger, fear, disgust, and surprise. Intensifiers like “very” or “extremely” and deintensifiers like “slightly” or “a bit” are taken into account, as well as repeated words that may strengthen emotion.
  • Semantic similarity: compares your text with prior entries to detect repeated ideas, related concepts, or patterns in phrasing using precomputed and real-time embeddings for efficiency.

Your saved entry

Each journal entry is assigned a unique SHA256-based ID (for example, 5edae32d) to track it privately without storing identifiable information. You are responsible for saving this ID. Entries are stored securely and visible only to authorized PeriDocs staff for moderation, safety review, or research into system performance. User text is never shared publicly.

Paraphrase Mirror: A simplified reflection of your text, often prefixed with "You wrote: …". It removes filler words, repeated words, and extra punctuation while prioritizing sentences with stronger emotional content. This feature is meant as a reflective mirror rather than a precise summary.

Repetition Multiplier: Measures similarity to previous entries based on sentence embedding comparisons. Values start at 1.0 and increase with repeated ideas or similar phrasing, highlighting patterns in your writing history.

Matched Excerpt & Source ID: Displays the most similar prior entry to your current text with its unique ID. Matches are calculated using embeddings and paraphrased to preserve privacy.

Average Sentence Length & Count: Provides information about the structure of your writing and helps users reflect on style, pacing, or complexity.

Embedding: Numeric vectors representing your text for semantic similarity detection. These vectors are used internally and are never shared publicly.

Emotion and sentiment features

PeriDocs detects nuanced emotional tone using a large lexicon of words and phrases for positive and negative feelings, intensifiers, deintensifiers, fillers, and colloquial expressions. The system accounts for context, repeated words, and negations to provide a balanced and normalized emotion profile. You receive a breakdown across six basic emotions and a sentiment score bucketed as negative, neutral, or positive. This helps users understand patterns in their emotional expression over time.

Privacy and safety

PeriDocs adheres to strict privacy protocols. Each submission receives a public 8-character hash ID for general browsing and a pseudonymized back-end identifier derived from your IP using HMAC for analytics and rate-limiting. The server sees your IP on each request, but human staff do not. Raw IPs are encrypted and stored only if a submission triggers a verified safety or legal escalation. Decrypted records are accessible only to authorized personnel, and all actions are logged. Users may request content removal by emailing peridocs@protonmail.com with the entry ID. Aggregated anonymized data may persist for internal analytics.

Crisis detection

Submissions indicating risk of harm to self or others are flagged immediately. Users see a safety notification directing them to professional help rather than saving the entry. In the U.S., dial 911 for emergencies or 988 for the Suicide & Crisis Lifeline. Outside the U.S., users are referred to local emergency services.

Ethical and design philosophy

PeriDocs prioritizes transparency, accountability, and ethical reflection. Algorithms, repetition detection, paraphrase mirrors, and crisis flags respect user privacy while providing meaningful educational insights. The interface avoids addictive engagement patterns and encourages careful exploration, reflection, and depth. PeriDocs’ name is inspired by Polanyi’s Paradox: much human knowledge is tacit and cannot be fully formalized. The system bridges algorithmic detection and nuanced understanding without pretending to replace human judgment.

Contact
