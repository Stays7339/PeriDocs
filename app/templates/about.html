About PeriDocs - updated 5 January 2026

Ethical and design philosophy

PeriDocs’ name is inspired by Polanyi’s Paradox: much human knowledge is tacit and cannot be fully formalized. PeriDocs bridges algorithmic detection and nuanced understanding without pretending to replace human judgment. PeriDocs itself is intended to serve as an intentional, accessible, and easily navigable space for users to explore our database and find actionable, AND situationally-relevant information that effectively applies to their unique struggles and experiences. At PeriDocs, we recognize and deeply admire the collective effort put in by the multitude of individuals and groups aiming to foster wellbeing regardless of personal affiliation. It is our mission to uplift their hard work by suggesting relevant discussions to the people who might want to see them, while avoiding chasing algorithm engagement. PeriDocs prioritizes transparency and ethical reflection. The interface tries to avoid addictive engagement patterns and encourages careful exploration, reflection, and depth. Our inspectable software encourages user privacy while providing meaningful educational insights. The platform does not generate therapeutic advice or diagnoses. Instead, it analyzes submitted text and finds relevant, previously verified material to help users interpret or contextualize their own writing.

System limitations

PeriDocs is not a mental health service and does not replace professional care. Analysis operates only on the text you submit and cannot infer context outside the words written. Similarity, sentiment, and entity detection are algorithmic and intended for educational reflection only. Results should never be relied upon for clinical or urgent decisions. Algorithms may be fallible and reflect patterns in your writing rather than absolute truths.

How analysis works

Imagine PeriDocs as a sprawling city built entirely from ideas, words, and emotions. Each entry you submit is like a new citizen arriving in this city, carrying a unique combination of thoughts, feelings, and expressions. The system’s first task is to understand what your citizen seem to be, in abstract terms; your words are transformed into thousands of numeric coordinates called embedding vectors.

Think of these vectors as the practically non-reversible anonymized versions character traits of the ideas within your writing. Embedding Vectors capture the meaning, tone, and subtle relationships between words and phrases, so the system can compare them to everyone else in the city.


These citizens don’t wander randomly around the city; they're guided into groups commonly called centroids, which are like areas of the city where similar ideas live nearby each other. Centroids keep and track the identity that its residents and commuters give it, meaning the neighborhood remembers its history and evolves carefully over time as more entries arrive. We like to use shorthand to remember this idea: neighborhood of nuance emotion (NNE) . Before a new NNE is officially recognized, it exists in a probationary stage called a precentroid, which allows the system to test its boundaries and determine if it truly represents a unique cluster of thought.


To help with placement, the system uses Software-auto-added journal entries (SAAJEs) as a way to suggest "addresses” for new "citizens". A SAAJE is not the literal words from your journal entry; rather, a SAAJE is a fingerprint derived from your entry’s embedding vectors that shows which centroids your ideas align with most closely. Think of it like a postal system: your mail (entry) is sorted by the relation of words to one another, not by the verbatim words themselves. This supports real-world privacy within the PeriDocs system while still guiding your thoughts into the right neighborhood. SAAJEs are subject to in-house human review where applicable for quality and moderation. This helps us with our goal of making sure neighborhoods grow in a meaningful, coherent way.


Once in a NNE, each citizen contributes to the evolving story of the neighborhood. The PeriDocs system continuously monitors these clusters, and if a neighborhood becomes too large or far spread out from citizen-to-citizen, the PeriDocs system may suggest splitting and properly establishing each NNE, much like a city adjusting its district as the population changes. Every large-level change is logged in a permanent internal audit trail, so the "city’s" big-picture history is never lost, and relationships between ideas remain transparent and traceable.


Your saved entry

Every citizen (an entry) in the PeriDocs city receives a public, unique 8-character ID based on SHA256 hashing (for example, 5edae32d). This is your key to accessing your entry later, while also reducing the amount and intensity of personally identifiable information about you. Raw text from your entry is never shared publicly; instead PeriDocs automatically removes any personally identifiable information that it detects for moderation, safety review, or research into how the city grows and functions.


When the system finds a similar citizen already living in the city, it provides a matched excerpt and source ID. These matches are paraphrased summaries to preserve privacy, highlighting semantic relationships without exposing raw words. This lets you see where your entry fits within the larger community, giving insight into recurring ideas or emotional patterns.


Behind the scenes, the embedding vectors are kept intact. These allow PeriDocs to detect nuanced relationships between entries/citizens across the entire city, providing semantic linking for reflection, pattern recognition, and educational insight, all while consistently performing some redaction on personally indentifiable information so that its never stored in the vector in the first place; other than that, vectors are never flattened, truncated, or shared publicly.

Emotion and sentiment features

Every citizen brings emotions with them, and PeriDocs maps these emotional footprints across the city. The system uses embeddings from open-source models to detect subtle shades of feeling, recognizing context, intensity, and recurring emotional patterns in your writing. PeriDocs doesn't generate advice or alter your words. The system focuses on showing you how the way you express yourself in long-form writing relates to the writing that's already out there on the open internet.


Each entry’s contextual signature is connected to these neighborhoods of nuanced emotion. For example, a district might represent recurring feelings of frustration or joy, or complex combinations that appear repeatedly across entries. When the system links your post to these centroids, it helps you recognize patterns in the writing you submitted, offering a bird’s-eye view of how common life-experiences and thought clusters ebb and flow. All of this happens without without ever using generative AI.


This mapping lets you explore the "City of PeriDocs" and its well-labeled archived of ideas and anecdotes and thoughts in a way that is richly detailed in whats oberserved in the writing of the entry users have submitted. Over time, you can see how your journal entries interact with neighborhoods of nuance emotion (NNEs) and reflect on the evolving landscape of thoughts and apparent themes from writing across time and across the open internet!

Crisis detection

Submissions indicating risk of harm to self or others are flagged immediately. Users see a safety notification notifying the presence of a potential crisis entry. In addition, users are reminded of the existence of emergency services local to their area. See our privacy policy for details about what information is stored for crisis entries.
