PeriDocs Privacy Policy

Effective Date: 3 November 2025

PeriDocs ("the Service") is committed to transparency and privacy. This document outlines what information we collect, how it is used, and how we protect the Users of the Service ("Users"). Cross-reference our Terms of Service for licensing and content usage.

1. Service Data Collection

When Users submit content, the User's computer (client-side) temporarily processes their network address (IP) to protect the service from abuse and comply with legal obligations if a threat is detected. The server (the Service's computers) sees the IP on every request, but raw IPs are never exposed to staff or developers under normal operations. Routine logging uses a pseudonymized HMAC hash of the IP, never storing raw IPs. These pseudonyms enable rate-limiting, detection of repeated submissions, and operational analytics without compromising privacy. Raw IPs are only accessible to authorized legal operations personnel in cases of crisis or legal escalation.

2. Text Analysis and Internal Metrics

Submitted text is processed to generate internal numeric representations (embeddings), semantic similarity comparisons, and sentiment or emotional tone metrics using curated lexicons and NLP models. These analyses are used solely for:

  • Providing educational and reflective insights to Users
  • Detecting repeated ideas or related content in your writing history
  • Generating anonymized, aggregate statistics for system performance and research
These representations cannot be traced back to individual Users and are never shared publicly or used for profiling, marketing, or clinical judgment.

3. Crisis and Safety Escalations

If a submission indicates potential harm to self or others, the system may flag it. Relevant ephemeral logs are copied into a secured incident file. The originating raw IP may be encrypted with a private asymmetric key and stored securely on servers fully managed by PeriDocs. Only authorized legal operations personnel can decrypt these records and share them with law enforcement when legally required. Every decryption or escalation action is recorded in an auditable log, including timestamp, reason, and actor. Escalation records are retained until resolution or as required by law. For users outside the U.S., local emergency services are referenced for guidance, while U.S. users are directed to 911 or 988.

4. Data Minimization and Retention

Routine logs of pseudonymized IPs and ephemeral system activity are retained for 30 days and automatically purged. Encrypted escalation records are retained until resolution or legal retention requirements. No behavioral telemetry, analytics profiles, or marketing data are collected. Entries themselves are stored perpetually unless a user requests removal by emailing peridocs@protonmail.com with their saved entry ID, at which point the license granted in TOS section 3 is revoked.

5. Local Storage and Sessions

Browser storage is minimal and ephemeral, used only for session tokens, dark mode preferences, or temporary cooldown timers. Session tokens are not linked to personal information.

6. Legal Compliance and Jurisdiction

PeriDocs complies with lawful requests from authorities in the jurisdictions where Users of the Service reside. Escalated data may be disclosed only when required to prevent imminent harm or satisfy legal obligations. This Privacy Policy is governed by the laws of Atlanta, Georgia, USA and applicable international privacy regulations.

7. Contact

For privacy, safety inquiries, or to request content removal, contact peridocs@protonmail.com. Verified safety reports and removal requests will be addressed in compliance with legal obligations.
