PeriDocs Privacy Policy

Effective Date: 5 January 2026

PeriDocs (“PeriDocs,” “we,” “us,” or “the Service”) provides an educational journaling and analysis platform. This Privacy Policy explains what personal data is processed, how it is processed, and the rights available to Users under applicable data protection laws. This document must be read together with our Terms of Service, which governs licensing, acceptable use, and deletion.

1. Scope and Governing Principles

PeriDocs is designed around the principles of data minimization, determinism, auditability, and human oversight. Personal data is processed only where necessary to operate the Service, comply with legal obligations, and maintain system integrity. No personal data is processed for advertising, behavioral profiling, or resale.

Where applicable, PeriDocs processes personal data in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the laws of the State of Georgia, United States of America. Users subject to CPPA legislation may have additional rights.

2. Categories of Data Processed

2.1 Account and Access Data

PeriDocs processes basic account identifiers such as email address and internal account IDs. Authentication data is used solely to provide account access and is not shared or repurposed.

2.2 User-Submitted Journal Entries

Users may submit free-form journal entries. These entries may contain personal or sensitive information depending on what the User chooses to write. PeriDocs does not require or solicit health, medical, or diagnostic information.

2.3 Derived Analytical Data

To operate the Service, PeriDocs generates internal analytical representations of journal entries, including semantic embeddings, similarity scores, centroid membership, and related metadata.

In accordance with applicable data protection laws, PeriDocs treats derived analytical data as personal data where it is reasonably linkable to a User or their content.

Embeddings are immutable numeric vectors generated using an offline-cached language model. They are never recomputed, never modified, and never reused for unrelated purposes.

2.4 Technical and Network Data

The Service necessarily processes network metadata such as IP addresses to deliver requests, protect against abuse, and ensure system security.

Under normal operations:

  • Raw IP addresses are not visible to staff or developers
  • Routine logs store only pseudonymized IP representations using keyed HMAC
  • Pseudonymized identifiers are used for rate-limiting and abuse detection only

Raw IP addresses may be encrypted and retained only in narrowly defined legal or safety escalation scenarios, as described below.

3. Purpose of Processing

Personal data is processed solely for the following purposes:

  • Operating and maintaining the PeriDocs Service
  • Providing requested educational and reflective analyses
  • Maintaining deterministic, auditable system behavior
  • Preventing abuse, fraud, or system misuse
  • Complying with legal obligations

PeriDocs does not process personal data for advertising, behavioral tracking, marketing analytics, or automated decision-making with legal or similarly significant effects.

4. Centroids, Moderation, and Human Oversight

Journal entries may be grouped into human-approved semantic clusters (“Centroids”) as described in PeriDocs’ published technical specification. All centroid creation, approval, rejection, splitting, and labeling decisions require explicit human moderation.

Software-generated suggestions never create authoritative categories on their own and never override human decisions. All centroid evolution is auditable and deterministic.

5. Crisis and Legal Escalations

If a submission indicates credible risk of harm or triggers a legal obligation, the system may generate a restricted incident record.

In such cases:

  • Relevant data may be copied into a secured incident file
  • Raw IP addresses may be encrypted using asymmetric encryption
  • Access is limited to authorized legal operations personnel
  • Every access, decryption, or disclosure is logged with timestamp, reason, and actor

Incident records are retained only as long as necessary to resolve the escalation or satisfy legal retention requirements.

6. Data Retention

Data retention follows strict minimization rules:

  • Pseudonymized operational logs are retained for up to 30 days
  • Encrypted escalation records are retained only as legally required
  • Journal entries and derived analytical data persist until deletion is requested or required by law

7. Deletion and Erasure Rights

Users may request deletion of their personal data through the account interface or by contacting peridocs@protonmail.com.

Upon a verified deletion request, PeriDocs will permanently delete:

  • User journal entries
  • Associated embeddings and similarity data
  • Centroid associations attributable to the User
  • Related metadata, except where retention is legally required

Deletion is irreversible and may permanently disable features associated with the User’s account. PeriDocs does not require Users to supply entry identifiers to exercise deletion rights.

8. Local Storage and Sessions

The Service uses minimal browser storage for session management and interface preferences. Session tokens are short-lived and are not used for tracking across services.

9. International Users

PeriDocs is operated from the United States. Personal data may be processed on servers located in jurisdictions different from the User’s residence, subject to appropriate safeguards.

10. Changes to This Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated to reflect changes in law or system behavior. Material changes will be reflected by updating the effective date.

11. Contact

Privacy or data protection inquiries may be directed to peridocs@protonmail.com.
